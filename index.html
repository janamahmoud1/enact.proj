RIVAQUA

is a project aimed at developing one of the most essential elements of life. The project is based on transforming the process and technology of water filtration into a more efficient, cost-effective, and environmentally friendly approach. By using a membrane made from low-cost materials and a key element that can be extracted in multiple ways, we have been able to produce a membrane that purifies water through forward osmosis—a more energy-efficient and sustainable method compared to reverse osmosis. Our primary goal is to facilitate access to clean water across Egypt in a simple and efficient manner while contributing to the increase of financial resources and per capita water share.

Problem:

Additionally, Egypt holds 97.5% of the world's saline water, so it was essential to take advantage of this resource. Although there are many desalination plants in Egypt, they consume around 2.5 to 3.5 kilowatts per hour, which is a high energy consumption. At RIVAQUA, we have managed to desalinate seawater at a lower cost and with less energy consumption. Our goal is to help more villages in need of clean water and to be part of the country's plan to solve the water crisis by 2050.

process:

first : Hydroxyapatite, which can be extracted from eggshells or synthesized chemically in the laboratory.

1- Wash the eggshell with hydrochloric acid to remove protein or place the eggshell in the oven at 700°C.
2- Dissolve the powder resulting from burning in distilled water to obtain a 2(Ca(OH) solution.
3- Slowly add phosphoric acid to the 2(Ca(OH) solution while stirring.
4- A white precipitate forms.
5- Separate the precipitate by filtration.
6- Place the precipitate in the oven at 80°C until it dries.

ON laboratory:

Required Chemicals:
1-Calcium nitrate tetrahydrate (Ca(NO₃)₂·4H₂O)
2-Disodium hydrogen phosphate (Na₂HPO₄)
3-Ammonium hydroxide (NH₄OH)
4-Distilled water

preparation Steps:

Preparation of Calcium Nitrate Solution:
1-Dissolve 23.62 grams of calcium nitrate tetrahydrate in 100 mL of distilled water.
Preparation of Sodium Phosphate Solution:
2-Dissolve 8.5 grams of disodium hydrogen phosphate in 100 mL of distilled water. Mixing the Solutions:
3-Slowly add the sodium phosphate solution to the calcium nitrate solution while stirring continuously.
pH Adjustment:
-Use ammonium hydroxide to adjust the pH of the mixture to approximately 10.
Stirring and Heating:
1-Continue stirring at room temperature for 24 hours to allow hydroxyapatite formation.
Filtration and Washing:
2-Filter the resulting precipitate and wash it several times with distilled water to remove impurities.
Drying:
-Dry the precipitate in an oven at 100°C for 12 hours.

Detailed Preparation Method for the Membrane:

Step 1: Preparation of Hydroxyapatite Solution
-Add 20 g of hydroxyapatite powder to 80 mL of distilled water.
-Stir the mixture thoroughly to ensure uniform distribution of hydroxyapatite.
-Use an ultrasonic device or magnetic stirring to achieve complete dispersion.
Step 2: Adding the Binding Agent
-Dissolve 2 g of PVA (Polyvinyl Alcohol) or PAM (Polyacrylamide) in 20 mL of warm distilled water while stirring continuously.
-Once fully dissolved, slowly add the binding agent solution to the hydroxyapatite mixture while stirring.
-Continue stirring until a homogeneous solution with a suitable consistency is obtained.
Step 3: Membrane Formation
-Pour the mixture onto a glass mold or a flat surface, ensuring a thickness between 0.5 – 1 mm.
-Use a blade or a flat tool to evenly distribute the solution across the surface for uniform membrane formation.
Step 4: Drying
-Allow the membrane to air dry for 12-24 hours.
-After the initial drying, place the membrane in an oven at 60-80°C for 24 hours to ensure complete hardening.
Step 5: Testing the Membrane’s Efficiency
-Once the membrane is fully solidified, test it in a laboratory by placing it in a water desalination unit.
-Pass water containing salts and heavy metals through the membrane to evaluate its efficiency in removing impurities.
-Use water analysis devices to measure the percentage of salts and metals removed.
